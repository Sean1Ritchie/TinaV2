Our Heritage

Three Generations
of Weaving Heritage

Founded in the late 1940s by Coral Stephens and master weaver Sylvia Mantanga in Eswatini, Stephens Tapestry Studio has grown from a single gift loom into one of the world's most significant handwoven tapestry institutions. Today, under Christine Weavind's direction, 30 women weavers across Johannesburg and Eswatini continue a tradition of extraordinary collaboration with the world's leading artists.

The Process

Five Steps from
Fleece to Masterpiece

01

Carding & Spinning

Raw Eastern Cape mohair is hand-carded and spun by women in Eswatini homesteads, a process unchanged for decades.

02

Dyeing

Mohair's natural lustre holds dye with extraordinary vibrancy — the secret behind the tapestries' signature light-catching quality.

03

Cartoon Development

The artist's design is translated into a full-scale paper "cartoon" — the template from which all weaving is guided.

04

Charting the Colours

Hundreds of individually numbered colour charts are developed over weeks, mapping every colour transition in the artwork.

05

Weaving

Teams of up to six weavers work at vertical looms for one to six months, building the tapestry row by row with absolute precision.
